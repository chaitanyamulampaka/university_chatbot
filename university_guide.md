Siddhartha Academy of Higher Education - Comprehensive University Guide
This document provides a complete overview of the policies, procedures, programs, student life, and code of conduct for Siddhartha Academy of Higher Education, Vijayawada, a Deemed to be University under Section 3 of the UGC Act, 1956.

1. University Mission and Vision
1.1. Preamble
The University's admission policy is committed to maintaining high standards and promoting academic excellence across a diverse range of programs, including Engineering, Management, Arts and Commerce, Science, and Law. The policy is designed to attract talented and motivated students through a transparent, merit-based selection process that fosters innovation, critical thinking, and a culture of research. Special consideration is given to national diversity and inclusivity.

1.2. Core Objectives
Merit-Based Selection: Admit students based on academic performance and entrance test scores.

Inclusivity and Access: Provide fair opportunities to students from diverse backgrounds and ensure reservations as per government regulations.

Transparency and Fairness: Implement a clear, well-documented admission procedure with clear eligibility and selection guidelines.

Research and Innovation: Select candidates with the potential to contribute to academic growth and research.

4.2. Admission Categories (Category A & B)
Sanctioned seats are classified into Category A and Category B at the first-year level.

Category B seats constitute up to 10% of the total sanctioned intake. The remaining seats fall under Category A.

Eligibility criteria are the same for both categories.

Unfilled Category B seats are converted into Category A seats.

There are no Category B seats for Lateral Entry students.

4.3. Step-by-Step Admission Procedure
Notification: Admission notifications are published in daily newspapers and on the university website 60 days before admissions commence.

Registration: Candidates must register online via the university website. Prospectus and details are available online and through various media.

Entrance Examination: Conducted at various centers across India as determined by the AAC.

Results: Results are communicated to students via SMS and E-mail and are also published on the university website.

Counseling:

The counseling schedule will be displayed on the university website.

Candidates must appear in person on the specified date and time. Failure to do so results in forfeiture of the seat for that day, though they may appear for subsequent counseling if seats are available.

Candidates must produce all required original documents and one set of photocopies.

The prescribed fee must be paid on the same day of counseling. No extension of time will be permitted.

Spot Admissions: After all regular counseling phases are complete, any remaining vacancies will be filled through Spot Admissions. Notification will be placed on notice boards and the university website. Priority is given to candidates with an entrance test rank.

5. Eligibility Criteria & Exam Patterns
5.1. B.Tech (Regular - 4 Years)
Age: Minimum 17 years.

Academics: Passed Intermediate (10+2) with Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry.

Marks: Minimum 50% aggregate marks.

Entrance Exam: Qualifying score in JEE (Mains) / SEEE / APEAPCET.

SEEE-2025 Exam Pattern:

Mode: Offline/Online

Max Marks: 100

Duration: 2 hours

Subjects: Physics (30), Chemistry (30), Mathematics (40).

5.2. B.Tech (Lateral Entry)
Intake: Up to a maximum of 10% of the approved intake (supernumerary).

Academics: Diploma in the appropriate branch of engineering.

Marks: At least 50% marks in the diploma.

Entrance Exam: Must have a rank in the ECET entrance examination conducted by the A.P. State Government.

5.3. M.Tech
Academics: B.Tech in the respective engineering discipline from a UGC-recognized university.

Marks: At least 50% marks in the undergraduate degree.

Entrance Exam: Admission is based on GATE Score / APPGECET.

5.4. MBA
Academics: A bachelor's degree of 3 or 4 years duration in any stream from a UGC-recognized university.

Marks: Minimum 50% aggregate marks.

Entrance Exam: Qualifying score in SPCAT / APICET.

Other Criteria: Selection is also based on performance in a Group Discussion & Personal Interview.

Ineligibility: A candidate who has passed all subjects of a three-year degree course in one sitting is not eligible.

SPCAT Exam Pattern (Shared with MCA):

Mode: Offline/Online

Max Marks: 150

Duration: 2 hours

Sections: Analytical Ability (60), Mathematical Ability (40), Communication Ability (50).

5.5. MCA
Academics: A bachelor's degree of 3 or 4 years duration with "Mathematics" as one of the subjects at the Intermediate or Degree level.

Marks: Minimum 50% aggregate marks.

Entrance Exam: Qualifying score in SPCAT / APICET.

Ineligibility: A candidate who has passed all subjects of a three-year degree course in one sitting is not eligible.

5.6. B.Com & B.Sc
Age: Minimum 17 years.

Academics: Must have passed 10+2 or equivalent. For B.Sc, subjects must include Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry.

Marks: Minimum 50% aggregate marks.

5.7. LL.M (2 Years)
Academics: Must have passed a 3 or 5-year LL.B Degree from a UGC-recognized university.

Marks: Minimum 50% aggregate marks in LL.B.

Age: No age bar for admission.

5.8. Ph.D. (All Disciplines)
Registration URL: https://admissions.vrsiddhartha.ac.in/

Entrance Exam: A valid SRCAT score followed by an interview is mandatory for both full-time and part-time admissions.

Exemption: Candidates with a valid score in Joint CSIR-UGC NET, APSET, or APRCET are exempt from SRCAT but must still attend an interview for admission.

Engineering Ph.D. Eligibility: Master’s in Engineering/Technology or M.S. by Research with a minimum of 55% aggregate marks.

Other Ph.D. Eligibility: Master's in the relevant discipline with a minimum of 55% aggregate marks.

Admission Categories:

Full-Time: Must work on campus; receives a fellowship for 3 years as per norms.

Part-Time: Self-financed or sponsored candidates; does not receive a fellowship.

6.2. Scholarship Policy
Eligibility: Scholarships are offered based on merit and are limited to 30% of the intake in respective courses, granted on a first-come, first-served basis.

Continuation Criteria: To continue receiving the scholarship, students must maintain:

Minimum 75% attendance in the subsequent years.

Minimum 7.5 CGPA with no backlogs.

Discontinuation: The scholarship will be stopped if these conditions are not fulfilled.

6.3. Fee Payment & Refund Policy
Fee Payment: The full fee for the odd semester must be paid before the commencement of classwork. The full fee for the even semester must be paid before the last date prescribed for the odd semester examination fee. Failure to pay on time will lead to the cancellation of the student’s registration number.

Refund Policy: In case of cancellation of admission, the admission fee is non-refundable. The tuition fee will be refunded in accordance with UGC guidelines.

7. Student Code of Conduct & University Policies
7.1. Core Values & Purpose
The Student Code of Conduct aims to create a safe, inclusive, and supportive learning environment that promotes academic excellence, personal integrity, and community responsibility. Core values include upholding academic standards, ethical behavior, tolerance, honesty, and respect.

7.2. Student Expectations
General Conduct: Act with integrity, communicate respectfully, respect property and the rights of others, and follow instructions from University staff.

Academic Conduct: Uphold academic honesty, stay informed by reading official communications, and avoid actions that create unfair academic advantages.

Interpersonal Conduct: Treat all community members with dignity and respect, and refrain from discrimination or harassment.

7.3. Prohibited Conduct (Comprehensive List)
The following behaviors are strictly prohibited on campus:

Assault, harassment, bullying, or intimidation.

Endangering the health or safety of others.

Stealing, damaging, or misusing property.

Disrupting academic or extracurricular activities.

Unauthorized use of University facilities or resources.

Disorderly conduct, possession of weapons or explosives.

Smoking, consuming alcohol, or using illegal drugs.

Misuse of social media, unauthorized recording, or distribution of content.

Participation in illegal activities or strikes.

Unauthorized entry of outsiders into campus areas.

Organizing or participating in politically sponsored activities without authorization.

Acting in a derogatory manner towards women.

Membership in banned religious or terrorist groups.

Failing to carry a photo ID when requested by authorities.

Using mobile phones in classrooms or examination halls.

Disturbing classes while moving through corridors.

Loitering.

Not cooperating with the administration in maintaining cleanliness.

Storing or possessing firearms or other weapons.

Organizing meetings without permission from the competent authority.

Collecting money without permission from the competent authority.

Theft or abuse of University computers and electronic resources.

Rash driving that causes inconvenience to others.

Distributing unauthorized notices or possessing obscene materials.

Hacking University websites or servers.

7.4. Disciplinary Actions
Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in a range of actions, including but not limited to:

Written warnings or reprimands.

Probation or restriction of access to University facilities.

A fine equal to three times the replacement value for damage to University property plus 25% of that amount.

Suspension or expulsion from the University.

Change of hostels or expulsion from hostels.

Referral to a Malpractice Enquiry Committee for exam-related violations.

8. Student Welfare, Safety, and Grievance Redressal
8.1. Anti-Ragging Policy
The University enforces a strict zero-tolerance policy against ragging in compliance with UGC Regulations (2009). All students and their guardians must submit an anti-ragging affidavit at the time of admission. Violators face severe disciplinary action, including suspension, revocation of scholarships, exclusion from exams, or expulsion. An FIR may be filed in serious cases. The Anti-Ragging Committee investigates all complaints.

8.2. Eagle Club (Anti-Narcotics)
Established in collaboration with the Andhra Pradesh Government, the Eagle Club (Elite Anti-Narcotics Group for Law Enforcement) promotes a drug-free academic environment through awareness campaigns, workshops, and strict enforcement of anti-drug policies.

8.3. Sexual Harassment Policy
The University adheres to the Sexual Harassment (Prevention, Prohibition & Redressal) Act 2013. Any employee or student who feels harassed must submit a written complaint within 10 days to a member of the Anti-Sexual Harassment Committee. The Committee ensures confidentiality while investigating complaints.

8.4. Grievance Redressal Committees
Anti-Discrimination Committee: Addresses complaints of discrimination based on caste, gender, race, disability, and other factors.

OBC Committee: Ensures the welfare and fair representation of OBC students and staff, monitoring reservation policies and addressing grievances.

SC/ST Committee: Plans, coordinates, and looks after the welfare and capacity enhancement of students and staff from SC/ST sections.

Minority Committee: Promotes the welfare and representation of minority communities, ensuring equitable access and support.

Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) for Women: Addresses complaints involving female students and staff in compliance with national guidelines regarding sexual harassment.

Grievance Appeal Committee: Manages appeals related to academic matters.

8.5. Complaint Mechanism
Students can report concerns or violations through various confidential channels:

Online Reporting: A dedicated portal on the University website.

In-Person Reporting: Direct reporting at the student welfare office through designated grievance officers.
